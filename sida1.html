DJ CARLEN

DJ Carlen (Pontus Carlstedt) is a Swedish DJ, record producer and songwriter. He has sold over nine million albums and thirty million singles worldwide. In 2011, Carlen was voted as the number one DJ in the DJ Mag Top 100 DJs poll. In 2013, Billboard crowned his Nr.1 hit song as the number one dance-pop collaboration of all time. Born and raised in Stockholm, he released his first album, Me, Myself and I, in 2002. Later, he released Wokeup yesterday (2004) and Simple Life (2007). Pontus achieved mainstream success with his 2009 album One Love which included the hit singles "When Love Takes Over", "Gettin' Over You", "Sexy Bitch" and "Memories", the first three of which reached number one in the United Kingdom. The 2011 follow-up album, Nothing but the Beat, continued this success, containing the hit singles "Where Them At", "Little And Bad", "Without You", "Titanium" and "Turn Me On". He is among the first DJs to get into the electronic dance music (EDM) scene and is known as the "grandfather of EDM".
